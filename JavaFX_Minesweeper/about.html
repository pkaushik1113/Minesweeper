How To Play Minesweeper

Minesweeper is a game where mines are hidden in a grid of squares. Safe squares have numbers telling you how many mines are touching that square. You can use the number clues to solve the game by opening all the safe squares. If you click on a mine you lose the game.

In this version of Minesweeper, the first click is always safe. You open squares with the left mouse button and put flags on mines with the right mouse button. When you open a square that does not touch any mines, it will be empty and the adjacent squares will automatically open in all directions until reaching squares that contain numbers.

There is a label at the bottom of the screen that shows how many mines are remaining. The number of remaining mines is calculated based on the number of flags placed, so if a flag is incorrect it will affect the number of mines remaining. There is also a time elapsed label that shows how many seconds have passed since the beginning of the game.

The three difficulty levels are Beginner (8x8 with 10 mines), Intermediate (16x16 with 40 mines) and Advanced (16x31 with 99 mines). The game ends when all safe squares have been opened. You can also play a custom game by changing the number of mines and the size of the board, with a minimum of 1 mine and a maximum of (row)(col) - 1 mines.
